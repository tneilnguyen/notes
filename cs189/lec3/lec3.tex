\section{Perceptron Algorithm (cont'd)}
\begin{itemize}
\item linear decision function \(f(x) = w \cdot x\)
\item decision boundary \(\{x: f(x) = 0\}\)
\item sample points \(X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n \in \mathbb{R}^d\); classifications \(y_1, \dots, y_n = \pm 1\)
\item goal: find weights \(w\) such that \(y_iX_i \cdot w \geq 0\)
\item goal, rewritten: find \(w\) that minimizes \(R(w) = \sum_{i \in V} -y_iX_i \cdot w\)) where \(V\) is the set of indices \(i\) for which \(y_iX_i \cdot w < 0\).
\end{itemize}
Objects in \(x\)-space transform to objects in \(w\)-space:\\
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline
\(x\)-space & \(w\)-space\\
\hline
hyperplane: \(\{z: w \cdot z = 0\}\) & point: \(w\)\\
point: \(x\) & hyperplane: \(\{z: x \cdot z = 0\}\)\\
\hline
\end{tabular}\\
If we want to enforce inequality \(x \cdot w \geq 0\), that means:
\begin{itemize}
\item in \(x\)-space, \(x\) should be on the same side of \(\{z: z \cdot w = 0\}\) as \(w\)
\item in \(w\)-space, \(w\) should be on the same side  of \(\{z: x \cdot z = 0\}\) as \(x\)
\end{itemize}
